# Images and Quantityes
# 图像和数量

Our thinking is filled with assessments of quantity, an approximate or exact sense of number, amount, size, scale. In scientific work, both order-of-magnitude reasoning and precise measurements are pervasive.How are such quantities represented in visual expressions of ideas,experience, evidence? How are moving images, photographs, diagrams,maps, and charts to be scaled and labeled? And what makes images quantitatively eloquent?

我们的思维中充满了对数量的评估，包括对数字、数量、大小、规模的大致或精确感知。在科学研究中，数量级推理和精确测量都十分普遍。那么，在对观点、经验、证据的视觉表达中，这些数量是如何呈现的呢？动态图像、照片、图表、地图和曲线图应如何进行缩放和标注？又是什么让图像在数量表达上具有说服力呢？

Visual techniques for depicting quantities include direct labels (for example, the numerically labeled grids of statistical graphics, or, at left,dimensional tippods in architectural drawings); *encodings* (color scales);and *self-representing scales* (objects of known size ppearing in an image).Using all these methods, Josef Koudelka’s haunting and vehement photograph, *The Urge to See*, testifies to the empty streets during the 1968 Soviet invasion of Czechoslovakia that ended the Prague Spring of democratic reform. In the foreground, a watch documents the hour (direct label), as the shadows and gray light hint at the time of day (*encoding*), while in the distance Soviet tanks surround the national museum (self-representing scales, as many familiar objects in perspective demarcate the street and the photographer’s location).

用于描绘数量的视觉技巧包括直接标注（例如，统计图表中带有数字标注的网格，或者左侧建筑图纸中的尺寸三脚架）、“编码”（色彩尺度）以及“自我表征尺度”（图像中出现的已知大小的物体）。约瑟夫·寇德卡那幅令人难忘且充满强烈情感的摄影作品《观看的欲望》运用了所有这些方法，见证了1968年苏联入侵捷克斯洛伐克期间空无一人的街道，这场入侵终结了旨在民主改革的布拉格之春。在前景中，一块手表记录着小时（直接标注），阴影和灰暗的光线暗示着一天中的时间（“编码”），而在远处，苏联坦克包围着国家博物馆（自我表征尺度，因为透视中的许多熟悉物体划定了街道和摄影师的位置）。

Despite their quantifying scales and grids, maps resemble miniature pictorial representations of the physical world. To depict relations between any measured quantities, however, requires replacing the map’s natural spatial scales with abstract scales of measurement not based on geographic analogy.
